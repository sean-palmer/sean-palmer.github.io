Sean Palmer | CV

Java Developer Internship at Revenue Commissioners

January 2015 - July 2015

During the third year of my degree, I spent seven months at an internship in the Revenue Commissioners. I used Java for most of the internship, with some JavaScript for the frontend of my projects. This internship opened me up to many new technologies. I learnt and used the Spring Framework for all my projects. I also got exposure to Maven and JUnit among some other tools such as Jenkins and SonarQube. I was lucky to have a great team that taught me how to use these tools. I learned how to write unit tests as well as how to write testable code. I also worked on an intern led project for the HR department and created a tool to allow HR to manage and plan training resources. This tool is still in use and was rolled out to other office sites.

Software Developement Enginner at Workday

July 2016 - January 2018

In July 2016 I started as Software Engineer in the Implementation Tools department in Workday. I spent much of my time developing features using Workdays propietary language. I also worked on a few Java features. Outside of my normal work, I dedicated myself to helping my team and others to improve their pipelines and automation. I quickly became the "Jenkins" guy for my department, helping many teams and projects. I also became very interested in automation and reformed the way my team was doing QA. I also setup a QA community of practice within my department.

DevOps Engineer at Workday

February 2018 - Current

We setup a DevOps team within my department, with the goal of supporting the teams with their pipeline and operational issues. I was the first member and the team was created out of the work that I did to highlight the many issues we had. This team aims to solve all those issues as well as help teams to make their processes more efficient and autonomous.
